Edge Dislocation — ⟂

An extra half-plane of atoms is inserted into the lattice. Atoms above the slip plane are in compression; atoms below are in tension. The dislocation symbol ⟂ shows the orientation of the extra half-plane.

Burgers Circuit

Walk an equal number of lattice steps right, up, left, down around the dislocation core. In a perfect crystal the loop closes; here it fails to close by exactly one lattice spacing — that closure failure is the Burgers vector b.

| b | = a (one lattice spacing, in the slip direction)
⟂ Key feature: the Burgers vector is perpendicular to the dislocation line. Slip moves the extra half-plane sideways one atomic distance at a time.

Screw Dislocation — ⟳

Imagine cutting the crystal along a half-plane and sliding one face parallel to the cut. Atomic planes spiral around the dislocation line like a spiral staircase — every full circuit around the line steps up by one Burgers vector.

Helical Displacement

In linear elasticity, the displacement field along the dislocation line is a smooth helix:

uz(x,y) = (b / 2π) · arctan(y/x)

Each atomic plane that was originally flat becomes a single turn of the helix. The dislocation line is the central axis of the screw.

⟳ Key feature: the Burgers vector is parallel to the dislocation line. There is no "extra plane" — atoms remain on the original lattice planes, but those planes spiral.
